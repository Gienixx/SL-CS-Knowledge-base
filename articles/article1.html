Navigating and Reviewing Tickets in Zendesk

June 13, 2026 By: Arby Benito

Guide on how to locate and understand how Zendesk Works

Article Map

Overview

Our team uses Zendesk as our ticketing platform to manage and respond to user concerns efficiently. Zendesk allows agents to track requests, review ticket history, and manage ongoing conversations in one centralized location.

This guide explains how to locate and open tickets within Zendesk and understand the information displayed inside each ticket.

Accessing Your Assigned Tickets

Once logged into Zendesk, navigate to your assigned ticket queue.

Open tickets assigned to you are organized under your personal ticket bucket labeled:

(Agent Name) - Unsolved Tickets

This view displays all active tickets that still require action or resolution.

Understanding the Ticket List View

Inside your Unsolved Tickets bucket, each row represents an individual support request.

The ticket list contains the following information:

  • Subject - Brief description or title of the concern submitted.
  • Requester Name - Name of the user who created the request.
  • Ticket ID - Unique identifier assigned to the ticket.
  • Date Logged to Zendesk - Date and time when the ticket was created.
  • Most Recent Update - Timestamp of the latest activity on the ticket.
  • Assigned Agent - Name of the support agent currently handling the case.
  • Application Used - Indicates which platform the user is contacting from:
    • Eureka
    • SurveyPop
    • SurveySpin

Reviewing these fields helps agents quickly prioritize and identify tickets before opening them.

Opening a Ticket

To review the full details of a support request:

  1. Locate the ticket from your (Agent Name) - Unsolved Tickets list.
  2. Click anywhere on the ticket row.

Once selected, Zendesk will open the ticket details in a new working tab.

Ticket Layout Overview

After opening a ticket, Zendesk is divided into three primary panels:

  • Left Panel - Ticket details and user attributes
  • Middle Panel - Conversation and response area
  • Right Panel - User profile and interaction history

Left Panel - Ticket Details and Attributes

The left side panel contains ticket information and account-related fields used during investigation.

Ticket Information

  • Requester Name - Displays the name of the user who submitted the request.
  • Assignee - Shows the support agent currently assigned to the ticket.
  • Followers - Lists users following the ticket for visibility purposes. This field can generally be ignored during standard handling.
  • Tags - Displays the ticket tags associated with the case. Tags help categorize concerns and route tickets appropriately.
  • Priority - Indicates the urgency level of the request: Low, Normal, High, Urgent.

User and Account Information

  • Platform - Displays the device platform being used: Android, iOS, Web.
  • Concerns - Displays the category or issue associated with the user's request.
  • Account Status - Shows the current state of the account: Active, Banned, Deleted, Inbox Full, Unknown / No Account.
  • Device Location - Indicates the detected device region.
  • Country - Displays the country code.

Additional Verification and Financial Information

The following fields reference values available in the Admin Tool:

  • Fraud Score - Available in the Admin Tool under the User side table.
  • SMS Verified - Displays the user's SMS verification status.
  • Lifetime Earnings - Available in the Admin Tool under the Money side table.
  • Lifetime Revenue - Available in the Admin Tool under the Money side table.
  • SMS Number - Available in the Admin Tool under the Verification side table.

Middle Panel - Ticket Conversation View

The middle section is where agents review and respond to tickets.

Subject Line

At the top of the panel, the Subject field is displayed. This field is editable and may be updated when necessary to better reflect the concern.

Conversation Thread

Below the subject is the email conversation history.

  • Blue-Green Bubble - User messages
  • Grey Bubble - Support responses
  • Orange Bubble - Internal notes

Internal notes are visible only to internal teams and are not sent to users.

Response Composer

At the bottom of the conversation panel is the response editor.

  • Public Reply - Sends a message directly to the user.
  • Internal Note - Creates an internal note only and appears highlighted in orange.
  • To - Displays the recipient email address.
  • CC - Adds another recipient into the email thread.

Right Panel - User Profile and History

The right-side panel contains user-specific information.

  • User's Name
  • Pop-out icon that opens the user's Zendesk profile
  • User's Email
  • User's Local Time
  • User's Local Language
  • User Notes

Interaction History

At the bottom of the right panel is the Interaction History section. This area displays previous tickets created by the same user, including the current active ticket.

Reviewing interaction history helps identify repeat concerns, previous resolutions, and ongoing account patterns.

Summary

Zendesk serves as the central workspace for handling support requests. Agents begin by reviewing tickets in their Unsolved Tickets bucket, then investigate ticket details through the left panel, manage communication in the middle panel, and reference user history and profile details from the right panel.
